F1 - The Pinnacle of Motorsport

This data has been collected from Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA)

The United Kingdom is a cornerstone of Formula 1 history, hosting numerous races at iconic circuits like Silverstone, Brands Hatch, and Donington Park. British drivers have made significant contributions to the sport, and Silverstone, a fast and demanding circuit, remains one of the most anticipated races on the F1 calendar.
The Monaco Grand Prix is renowned for its prestige and allure. The race takes place on the narrow, winding streets of Monte Carlo, offering a unique challenge to drivers. Monaco is synonymous with glamour and history, attracting a worldwide audience and showcasing the opulence associated with Formula 1.
Italy has a rich Formula 1 heritage, hosting the historic Italian Grand Prix at Monza, one of the fastest tracks on the calendar. Monza has witnessed legendary battles and is known for its long straights and high-speed corners. Italian passion for motorsport and the success of Ferrari contribute to the country's strong association with Formula 1.
The Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps in Belgium is renowned for its challenging layout, including the iconic Eau Rouge-Raidillon complex. The Belgian Grand Prix is celebrated for its unpredictable weather and thrilling races. Spa-Francorchamps remains a favorite among drivers and fans alike.
Germany has a rich motorsport tradition and has hosted several Formula 1 races at iconic tracks like Hockenheimring and Nürburgring. Hockenheimring, with its long straights and tight corners, has witnessed numerous memorable moments, while the Nürburgring, also known as the "Green Hell," is a historic and demanding circuit.
The Australian Grand Prix, held in Melbourne at the Albert Park Circuit, marks the beginning of the F1 season. The event is known for its vibrant atmosphere and is a fan favorite. The Albert Park Circuit, a semi-street circuit, poses a mix of challenges, making it an exciting opener for the championship.

F1 Teams throughout the Years

As of the Japanese Grand Prix 2023. There have been 171 constructors who have participated in F1. Currently there are only 10 constructors participating in F1 and it has been the case since 2017. The main few that come to mind for even the general public are Ferrari, Mercedes, McLaren as they are well known in the automotive industry.

Teams have gone through changes in name due to some teams having financial struggles to stay within the sports such as Force India and others have a simple rename due to delegating their operations to a different department in their company like Renault.

What remains clear though is that Ferrari, Mercedes and Red Bull are the most consistent teams in the top 3 and this is due to their financial backing and their ability to attract the best drivers in the world.

The Historical Best F1 Teams

F1 is a sport that has been around for a long time and has seen many teams come and go. The teams that have been around for a long time have been able to accumulate a lot of points and wins. The top 5 teams are Ferrari, Mercedes, Red Bull, McLaren and Williams.

The teams that are not present currently in F1 such as Lotus, Renault, Brabham and Benetton have been bought out by other teams, and have been renamed to the current teams that are present in F1. However their iteration of the team has been deemed the most successful due to their dominance in their era of F1.

How many Drivers are Champion

The term "Champion" is used to describe a driver who has won the F1 World Championship. There have been 775 drivers who have participated in F1, with only 33 drivers who have won the F1 World Championship and 3 of them are still active in the sport.

The most successful drivers are Michael Schumacher and Lewis Hamilton with both winning 7 world championships.

The Nations that have Raced

United Kingdom has the most drivers with 164 drivers, followed by the United States with 158 drivers and then Italy with 99 drivers.

The most successful nation is the United Kingdom with 19 world championships, followed by Germany with 12 world championships and then Brazil with 8 world championships.
